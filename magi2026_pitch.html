The Problem

Clinical research coordination
is a reactive profession

Coordinators and QA leaders are managing an increasingly complex execution environment — more procedures per visit, more protocol amendments, tighter scheduling windows, and PIs stretched across more trials than ever. The entire quality infrastructure is built to find problems after they happen.

📄
Protocol Complexity Has Tripled
Phase III trials now generate 3× more data points than a decade ago — from 929K to 3.6M between 2012 and 2020. Each amendment adds new procedures, new windows, and new risk with no system to flag the impact. (Tufts CSDD, 2021)
🔍
Deviations Are Identified Retrospectively
QA oversight is structured around monitoring visits that happen after the fact. By the time a deviation is logged, the window for prevention has already closed.
👨‍⚕️
PI Oversight Gaps Are Invisible
Principal Investigators are managing more concurrent trials than ever. No system tracks whether a PI has the bandwidth to oversee an upcoming high-complexity visit.
⏱
Visit Window Pressure Builds Silently
Tight ±2-day scheduling windows with no buffer leave coordinators executing under pressure with no visibility into compounding risk before visit day.
🔄
CAPA Cycles Repeat Without Prevention
The same deviation types recur at the same sites, in the same visit types — because corrective action happens after the pattern is established, not before.
💡
Quality Culture Suffers Under Surveillance
Tools that score or monitor individuals create a punitive environment. What coordinators need is support — targeted, contextual, and non-threatening guidance.

Innovation Challenge · MAGI 2026

Proactive risk awareness
before the visit happens

Deviation Risk Radar is an AI-enabled early warning system that tells QA leaders and clinical research coordinators exactly where protocol deviations are most likely to occur — days before the visit — so they can intervene instead of react.

1
Non-patient data only — protocol documents, scheduling systems, CTMS, EDC query counts, and PI availability records
2
No individual performance scoring — all signals are site-level operational assessments, not personal evaluations
3
Explainable AI — every risk score includes a plain-language narrative explaining exactly why risk is elevated
4
Decision support — designed to inform human judgment, never to automate compliance or enforcement

The AI Pipeline

Six-stage analysis
before every visit

When a protocol is analyzed, the system runs through six sequential stages — each one feeding signal into the final composite risk score.

🔍
OCR Extraction
Parse protocol PDF, extract visit schedule, PI requirements, amendment history
📊
Deviation Patterns
Compare site rates to network norms across all visit types
📅
Visit Windows
Flag tight scheduling constraints and amendment-driven timeline changes
⚡
Workload Signals
Assess concurrent visit burden, open query backlog, coordinator experience
👨‍⚕️
PI Workload
Evaluate PI availability vs protocol-required hours, ranked by risk
🧠
Risk Score
Composite weighted score with full explainability narrative
